# Executive Summary

Effective workplace writing integrates clear organization, audience awareness, and analytical rigor, increasingly supported by responsible AI use. The CC1X08 Effective Writing for the Workplace (EWW) course materials emphasize these competencies through practical frameworks, situating coursework within the context of the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) to bridge foundational communication theory with real-world, impact-driven professional scenarios.

The curriculum covers the entire writing process, starting with understanding the Purpose, Audience, and Context ([[Foundations of Workplace Writing|PAC]]) of a document and applying the [[Foundations of Workplace Writing|5Cs of communication]] for clarity and professionalism. It progresses through structural strategies like the [[Structuring_and_Organizing_Content|LATCH method ]]for information organization and [[Structuring_and_Organizing_Content|effective use of headings]]. A significant focus is placed on enhancing reports with appropriate [[Utilizing_Visuals_in_Communication|visual aids]] and utilizing structured analytical frameworks, such as [[Analysis_Evaluation_and_Persuasion|SWOT and PESTLE]], to move from analysis to critical evaluation and persuasion.

The course also addresses the modern reality of AI in the workplace, teaching students to craft well-engineered prompts using the [[Prompts and More|CRAFT]] framework for research and drafting. Concurrently, it reinforces strict guidelines on academic integrity, including avoiding plagiarism, accurately citing sources using the [[Harvard Citations|Harvard referencing system]], and properly declaring AI tool usage through mandatory documentation.
