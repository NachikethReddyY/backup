# Concept Wiki

# [[EWW|5Cs of Communication]]
A checklist for effective workplace writing, requiring documents to be Complete, Concise, Clear, Courteous, and Correct. It serves as a quality control measure for content developed under the PAC Principle.

## Analysing
The process of examining individual elements of a subject to understand their interrelationships, serving as a factual basis for discussion or interpretation. It utilizes tools like [[Analysis_Evaluation_and_Persuasion#SWOT Analysis|SWOT]] Analysis and [[Analysis_Evaluation_and_Persuasion#PESTLE Analysis|PESTLE Analysis]] and precedes Evaluating.

## [[Prompts and More#The CRAFT Framework for AI Prompts|CRAFT Framework]]
An acronym for Context, Role, Audience, Format, and Tone, detailing the essential elements of a well-engineered AI prompt. It helps generate accurate AI responses without replacing human critical thinking or leading to Plagiarism.

## Evaluating
The concluding stage of analysis, involving evidence-based judgments about quality or effectiveness, often leading to recommendations. It builds upon Analysing and heavily relies on Persuasive Techniques.

## Headings and Sub-headings
Formatting elements used to logically organize content, visually convey hierarchy, and briefly describe subsequent text. They should be structured cohesively according to the LATCH Method.

## [[Structuring_and_Organizing_Content#LATCH Method|LATCH Method]]
A framework for organizing information based on Location, Alphabet, Time, Category, and Hierarchy, created by Richard Saul Wurman. It provides structural logic for deploying Headings and Sub-headings in reports.

## [[Executive_Summary|Executive Summary]]
Summarizes the importance of effective workplace writing, emphasizing organization, audience awareness, and analytical rigor. Highlights the integration of AI tools and academic integrity.

## [[Foundations_of_Workplace_Writing|Foundations of Workplace Writing]]
Explains the PAC Principle and the 5Cs of Communication. Lists types of workplace communication.

## [[Foundations_of_Workplace_Writing#PAC Principle|PAC Principle]]
An acronym for Purpose, Audience, and Context, forming the foundation for determining the content, organization, and language of any written communication. It works in tandem with the 5Cs of Communication.

## [[Analysis_Evaluation_and_Persuasion#Persuasive Techniques|Persuasive Techniques]]
Rhetorical strategies used to convince a reader, including establishing credibility, adopting an authoritative tone, and employing repetition. These are crucial in report conclusions to strengthen the impact of Evaluating.

## [[Analysis_Evaluation_and_Persuasion#PESTLE Analysis|PESTLE Analysis]]
A strategic tool for evaluating external macro-environmental factors: Political, Economic, Socio-cultural, Technological, Legal, and Environmental. It is often used with the external components (Opportunities and Threats) of a SWOT Analysis.

## [[Structuring_and_Organizing_Content#Richard Saul Wurman|Richard Saul Wurman]]
Creator of the TED conference and an information architect, credited with developing the LATCH Method, theorizing that all finite information organization falls into five categories.

## [[Executive_Summary#Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)|Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)]]
Global objectives set by the United Nations to address social, economic, and environmental challenges, serving as the core thematic focus and context for student research and reports.

## [[Analysis_Evaluation_and_Persuasion#SWOT Analysis|SWOT Analysis]]
A strategic framework for evaluating internal Strengths and Weaknesses, and external Opportunities and Threats. It is a primary tool for Analysing and is paired with PESTLE Analysis.

## [[Utilizing_Visuals_in_Communication#Visuals|Visuals]]
Non-textual communication elements like maps, graphs, tables, and infographics used to summarize data and clarify relationships. They must be appropriately labeled and integrated into the text to support both Analysing and Evaluating.
