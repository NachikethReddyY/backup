# Raw Capture:
This is my take on Jeff Sue's first day with his AI toolkit. He suggests starting with ChatGPT if a task has many moving parts and getting one wrong would break the whole thing. A quick way to test this is to ask each model to optimize a prompt for ChatGPT. ChatGPT will generate a longer, more detailed prompt because it can handle complexity.

You might consider outsourcing prompt formation to ChatGPT and then using Gemini for its multimodality. Gemini's superpower is its native ability to process all four data types: video, audio, images, and text. ChatGPT, Claude, and Grok use workarounds for audio and video, but Gemini can directly process these files.

Gemini's large context window allows it to handle lengthy video recordings, hour-long audio files, and entire slide decks simultaneously. For example, after a meeting, you could upload the video recording, slide deck, and a photo of a whiteboard to Gemini. You could then ask it to summarize the discussion, extract key points, and draft an email. Gemini is the only tool that can synthesize these three inputs in one go. Therefore, if your task involves video, audio, or massive files, Gemini is the best choice.

Claude's superpower lies in producing higher-quality first drafts. Claude's initial attempt is typically closer to completion than what you get from ChatGPT or Gemini. For coding, developers generally agree that Claude writes functional code on the first try more consistently than other alternatives. You could ask Claude and Gemini to write a functional Google Apps Script to remove speaker notes from Google Slides. While Gemini, being a Google product, might seem like the logical choice, Claude will likely get it right on the first try, whereas Gemini might require more guidance.

Regarding writing, unlike ChatGPT or Gemini, Claude seems to maintain tone consistency and handle creative iterations with fewer corrections. This is crucial for tasks like proofreading or refining YouTube transcripts or newsletters. If your final output needs to be polished code, emails, copy, or reports, Claude can help you achieve that in a few rounds.

Should you pay for a chatbot? Yes, unless you are a student. Pick one and pay for it. I recommend ChatGPT to get started. The difference between the free and paid versions is substantial, making the free version a significant handicap unless you cannot afford it. In that case, consider free Chinese models like DeepSeek Coder. Pay for one model and become proficient with it.

Perplexity's superpower is its ability to fetch accurate information quickly. Gemini, ChatGPT, and Claude are designed for reasoning, brainstorming, and writing. Perplexity, on the other hand, is built for rapid information retrieval. It serves as a replacement for traditional search engines, not just a general chatbot.

Lastly, there's NotebookLM. This tool is best for grounded research. Its superpower is that it only answers based on the sources you provide. It cannot hallucinate because it doesn't draw knowledge from external sources. A use case is uploading your draft alongside source materials before publishing and asking NotebookLM if the draft contradicts the sources. It can catch subtle discrepancies that other AIs might overlook. The obvious caveat is that the output's quality depends entirely on the sources you provide. If the sources are flawed, the output will be confidently incorrect. If accuracy is more critical than creativity, and you have source material to verify against, NotebookLM is the ideal tool.

These are the tools we've discussed. We should explore each of them in more detail. ChatGPT excels at obedience. Gemini is strong in multimodality. Claude is great for first drafts. Perplexity is ideal for search, and NotebookLM is useful for grounding answers. 

# Cleaned Notes:
- **ChatGPT**: Best for complex tasks requiring detailed prompts. Great for obedience and prompt optimization.
- **Gemini**: Handles multimodal inputs (video, audio, images, text) natively. Ideal for summarizing and synthesizing large files.
- **Claude**: Produces high-quality first drafts and functional code. Maintains tone consistency and requires fewer iterations.
- **Perplexity**: Functions as a search engine replacement, excelling in quick and accurate information retrieval.
- **NotebookLM**: Provides grounded research by answering only based on provided sources. Ideal for verifying accuracy against source materials.
