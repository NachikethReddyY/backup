# Context:
An overview of the best AI tools available in 2026 for various use cases, focusing on free and open-source options.

# Raw Capture:
- Large Language Models (LLMs): Open-weight models like Qwen 3.5, DeepSeek-V3.2, and LLaMA 4.
- Deep Learning Frameworks: PyTorch, TensorFlow, JAX, and their high-level libraries.
- Machine Learning: Foundational libraries like NumPy, SciPy, and Pandas, along with tools like Scikit-learn, XGBoost, and LightGBM.
- Vision & Image Generation: Tools like OpenCV, YOLOv8, and Stable Diffusion 3.
- Audio & Speech: Whisper, Wav2Vec2, and MusicGen.
- Agents & Orchestration: LangGraph, CrewAI, and LangChain.
- Vector Databases: Chroma, Milvus, and FAISS.
- Data Processing: Docling, Unstructured, and Crawl4AI.

# Cleaned Notes:
- **LLMs**: Open-weight models like Qwen 3.5 and LLaMA 4 are ideal for local deployment and fine-tuning.
- **Deep Learning Frameworks**: PyTorch is dominant for research, TensorFlow for enterprise, and JAX for numerical computing.
- **Machine Learning**: Scikit-learn, XGBoost, and LightGBM are essential for classical ML tasks.
- **Vision & Image Generation**: OpenCV and YOLOv8 are excellent for computer vision, while Stable Diffusion 3 is great for text-to-image generation.
- **Audio & Speech**: Whisper and Wav2Vec2 are top choices for speech-to-text, while MusicGen excels in audio generation.
- **Agents & Orchestration**: LangGraph and CrewAI are leading frameworks for agentic AI workflows.
- **Vector Databases**: Chroma and FAISS are lightweight and free, ideal for semantic search and RAG.
- **Data Processing**: Tools like Docling and Unstructured are best for document preprocessing.
