# ADS Recruitment — Speaker Script
**Event:** Freshman Orientation Program
**Duration:** ~5 minutes
**Deck:** ADS_Recruitment.pptx (14 slides)

---

## Slide 1 — "Build something the world hasn't seen yet."

Welcome, everyone. I'm going to keep this short — because the people who build things don't waste time. In the next five minutes, I want to show you one of the best decisions you can make at Poly.

---

## Slide 2 — "You chose engineering for a reason."

You didn't come here just to pass modules. You came here because you wanted to make something. The question is — where do you start? That's where we come in.

---

## Slide 3 — "We are the builders."

ADS is NTU's only CCA dedicated to building on Apple platforms. We're not a club that just watches tutorials. We ship actual apps. Real products. Real skills.

---

## Slide 4 — "1.8 billion Apple devices."

Apple's ecosystem is one of the most valuable platforms in the world. Over 1.8 billion active devices. If you can build for it, doors open. And we give you the skills to do exactly that — starting now.

---

## Slide 5 — "What we do."

We're built around three pillars. You **learn** the craft through hands-on workshops. You **build** by shipping actual things. And you **connect** — with people in the industry, and with peers who push you further.

---

## Slide 6 — "Workshops you actually learn from."

We start from zero — SwiftUI, Xcode, all of it. No prior Apple experience needed. By the time you leave your first workshop, you'll have something running on a simulator. That's not theory. That's real.

---

## Slide 7 — "48 hours."

Every year we run a hackathon. 48 hours, a team, and a blank Xcode project. You'll be exhausted, caffeinated, and more proud of something you made than anything you hand in for a grade. It's that good.

---

## Slide 8 — "You won't build alone."

University can feel competitive and isolating. ADS is different. You'll be surrounded by people who want to see you succeed — seniors who've been in your seat, and friends who become your first founding team.

---

## Slide 9 — "Real people. Real careers. Real advice."

We bring in real people — engineers at Apple, founders, designers. They talk about their path, their mistakes, and what they wish they'd started earlier. Spoiler: almost all of them say they wish they'd built more.

---

## Slide 10 — "Early Bird Recruitment Is Open."

We're recruiting right now — and we specifically save spots for freshmen. You don't need to have built anything before. We'll teach you everything. But the early cohort is limited, so this is your moment.

---

## Slide 11 — "Why join in Year One?"

Year One is when habits form, when you choose what kind of student you're going to be. The people who join CCAs in Year One consistently say it was one of the best decisions they made. Don't let the semester fill up first.

---

## Slide 12 — "What you get."

Here's what's concrete. Workshops every semester, a hackathon, a real portfolio, mentors, and an early bird membership that gets you priority access to everything we do. It's a lot — for very little friction to get in.

---

## Slide 13 — "How to join."

Three steps. Scan the QR — it's going up right now. Fill in your details. And then just show up. That's genuinely all it takes. You don't need to write code before your first session.

---

## Slide 14 — "The best time to build is now."

That's it. No fluff, no gimmicks. We're a group of people who love building, and we want you in the room. The best time to start is in Year One. And the best place to start is with us. See you there.

## Presenter Tips

- **Slide 2:** Pause after "What are you going to do with it?" — let it sit for a beat.
- **Slide 7:** Say "48 hours." and stop. Don't rush past it. The silence sells it.
- **Slide 10:** Slow down here. This is the turn — the audience shifts from listening to deciding.
- **Slide 13:** Pull up the QR while you're still talking. Don't wait until you finish the line.
- **Slide 14:** Don't end with "any questions?" — end on "See you there." and hold the slide.
